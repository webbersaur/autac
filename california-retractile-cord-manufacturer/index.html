Industries We Serve Across California

California is the largest manufacturing economy in the United States, with six distinct clusters that all generate steady demand for industrial retractile, curly, and coiled cordage. Aerospace and space launch dominate the South Bay and Antelope Valley. Medical-device manufacturing concentrates in Orange County, San Diego, and the Bay Area. Silicon Valley anchors a globally significant semiconductor cluster. EV and advanced-automotive operations span Fremont, Irvine, and Newark. Hollywood and Burbank host the world's largest entertainment-production infrastructure. And the Central Valley produces a sizable share of the country's processed food, dairy, beverage, and agricultural output. Each cluster carries its own engineering requirements — current rating, jacket compound, flex life, shielding, environmental exposure — and the Autac product line maps directly to all six.

Aerospace, Defense & Space

Southern California hosts the densest aerospace and space-launch ecosystem on the planet. SpaceX manufactures Falcon and Starship hardware in Hawthorne. Lockheed Martin Skunk Works runs advanced programs out of Palmdale. Northrop Grumman operates from El Segundo and Redondo Beach. Boeing Satellite Systems builds spacecraft in El Segundo and assembles defense aircraft in Long Beach and Huntington Beach. Raytheon, JPL/NASA in Pasadena, Anduril in Costa Mesa, Relativity Space in Long Beach, and Rocket Lab in Long Beach all share a common engineering challenge: durable, traceable power and signal delivery to test stands, assembly fixtures, clean-bench work areas, and ground support equipment that must satisfy AS9100, ITAR, and federal supplier-quality requirements.

Typical California applications in this segment:

  • Rocket-engine test-stand instrumentation and power feeds
  • Satellite and spacecraft clean-bench assembly drops
  • Ground support equipment (GSE) for launch and integration sites
  • Missile and aerospace assembly tool drops on long, complex lines
  • ITAR-compliant supplier-managed cordage with US-only supply chain

Recommended Autac products: TPR-jacketed retractile cords in 14 AWG and 12 AWG, 3-conductor / 15A configurations cover most assembly-line drops; custom retracted lengths from 18" to 36" are standard. Step up to 10 AWG / 20A for higher-current test-stand and GSE applications. Browse the Retractile Cords hub for stock part numbers, or use the Build Your Cord tool to specify a custom configuration for your program. Autac is a US-only manufacturer with a US supply chain, which simplifies ITAR-program supplier qualification.

Medical Device & Life Sciences

California is one of the largest medical-device manufacturing states in the country, with major clusters in Orange County, San Diego, and the Bay Area. Edwards Lifesciences builds heart valves and hemodynamic monitoring systems in Irvine. Stryker manufactures surgical instruments and endoscopy systems across San Jose and Salinas. Abbott's diagnostic and vascular divisions operate from Alameda, Sylmar, and Temecula. Illumina builds genomic sequencers in San Diego. Intuitive Surgical manufactures the da Vinci robotic surgery platform in Sunnyvale. Masimo (Irvine), ResMed (San Diego), Thermo Fisher, and Boston Scientific round out a deep regional supplier base. Medical-device manufacturing carries its own constraints: ISO Class 7 and Class 8 cleanroom controls, sterilization-compatible jacket compounds, FDA Design History File traceability, and biocompatibility documentation.

Typical California applications in this segment:

  • ISO Class 7/8 cleanroom assembly-line tool drops
  • Surgical robot teach-pendant and end-effector signal cables
  • Sterilization-compatible cord assemblies for reusable equipment
  • Diagnostic and imaging equipment power and control cables
  • Wash-down medical-device assembly and packaging lines

Recommended Autac products: Our Auta-Prene thermoset rubber and TPR jacket compounds resist the cleaning and sterilization chemistries common in medical manufacturing. For signal applications, 18 AWG and 16 AWG shielded retractile and curly configurations support millions of flex cycles in surgical-robot teach pendants and end-effector cables. Browse Curly Cords for high-flex signal builds and Coiled Cords for shielded multi-conductor configurations. Lot traceability documentation is available for FDA Design History File submissions.

Semiconductor & Cleanroom Manufacturing

Silicon Valley remains the global center of semiconductor capital-equipment manufacturing. Applied Materials in Santa Clara, Lam Research in Fremont, and KLA in Milpitas are the three largest US wafer-fab equipment makers, all headquartered within twenty miles of each other. Intel, AMD, Nvidia, and ASML operate engineering and assembly facilities across the Bay Area. Semiconductor capital equipment carries some of the most demanding cordage requirements in any industry: ESD-safe materials, ultra-clean jacket compounds, vacuum-compatible outgassing characteristics, high-flex life for wafer-handling robots, and EMI shielding for variable-frequency-drive and RF-process environments.

Typical California applications in this segment:

  • Wafer-handling robot cables with high-flex life and ESD properties
  • FOUP-loader and overhead-track power and signal feeds
  • Metrology and inspection-tool drop cords for fab equipment
  • Sub-fab utility connections for vacuum, abatement, and chemical-delivery skids
  • Process-tool integration cables for VFD- and RF-heavy environments

Recommended Autac products: 18 AWG and 16 AWG shielded retractile and curly configurations dominate wafer-handling and metrology applications, with retracted lengths from 12" to 24". For ESD-safe and low-outgassing requirements, contact engineering directly to specify a jacket compound that meets your fab's particle and contamination spec. Browse Curly Cords for high-flex signal builds and Coiled Cords for shielded multi-conductor assemblies suited to VFD-rich environments.

EV & Advanced Automotive

California anchors the US electric-vehicle industry. Tesla manufactures Model 3, Model Y, Model S, and Model X at the Fremont assembly plant and battery cells at its Lathrop and Sparks-adjacent facilities. Rivian engineering and product development operates from Irvine. Lucid Motors builds the Air and Gravity in Newark and Casa Grande. Faraday Future, Karma Automotive, ChargePoint, EVgo, and Stem round out the EV-and-charging cluster. Battery and EV manufacturing carries its own electrical requirements: high-current assembly drops for cell-pack assembly, drive-cycle dynamometer cabling, and high-voltage test-cell power feeds rated for repeated thermal and electrical cycling.

Typical California applications in this segment:

  • Battery-cell and pack-assembly tool drops on moving lines
  • Drive-cycle dynamometer power feeds for vehicle and powertrain testing
  • High-voltage test-cell cabling for battery and motor validation
  • EV-charger production line tools and inspection-station power
  • Battery thermal-test station instrumentation and power feeds

Recommended Autac products: 10 AWG / 20A retractile configurations handle the higher-current loads typical of battery assembly and drive-cycle test cells. TPR and Auta-Prene jackets resist the cutting fluids, coolants, and battery-electrolyte residues common in EV production. Browse the Retractile Cords hub for stock high-current configurations, and use Build Your Cord to specify retracted length, extension ratio, and termination for your line.

Entertainment, Film & Broadcast Production

Los Angeles is the largest entertainment-production hub in the world. Walt Disney Studios (Burbank), Universal Studios, Paramount Pictures, Warner Bros. Broadcast networks (ESPN, Fox, CBS, NBC) maintain large LA-based studio and field operations. Production environments share a common requirement: power and signal cordage that retracts cleanly out of camera frames and trip hazards, holds up to constant rolling and rigging, and color-codes to plant-and-studio standards.

Typical California applications in this segment:

  • Sound-stage overhead lighting drops on grid and perm trusses
  • Camera dolly, jib, and pedestal power and signal retracts
  • Location and grip-truck power distribution cords
  • Broadcast-cart, control-room, and OB-van signal and intercom cords
  • Retractile mic, headset, and IFB cables for talent and crew

Recommended Autac products: Curly cords in standard 18 AWG to 14 AWG configurations with TPR or PVC jackets are the workhorse for mic, headset, and signal applications. For higher-current sound-stage and lighting drops, 12 AWG retractile configurations with custom retracted lengths from 24" to 48" pull cleanly out of the work area when not in use. Browse Curly Cords for signal builds and Cord Sets for custom-terminated production-ready assemblies. Conductor color coding for production-standard wiring schemes is documented on our Color Charts.

Food, Beverage & Agricultural Processing

California's Central Valley and North Coast wine country together produce more processed food, dairy, beverage, and agricultural output than most countries. Foster Farms, Driscoll's, Wonderful Company (Lost Hills almonds and pistachios), Dole, Frito-Lay (Bakersfield and Rancho Cucamonga), Hilmar Cheese, Blue Diamond Growers, Sun-Maid, and dozens of smaller co-packers run packaging, processing, and palletizing lines around the clock. E&J Gallo, Constellation Brands, Wente Vineyards, and Jackson Family Wines anchor Napa, Sonoma, and Lodi wine production. These operations share a common environment: wash-down sanitation cycles, chemical and food-acid exposure, dust and particulate from grain and produce handling, and zero tolerance for line downtime during harvest and peak runs.

Typical California applications in this segment:

  • Wash-down rated retractile drops over packaging and filling lines
  • Palletizer, case-packer, and depalletizer tool power
  • Conveyor-line inspection station and torque-tool power
  • Dairy, juice, and beverage plant wash-down and CIP-compatible cordage
  • Harvest-season co-packer field service and rebuild kit cords

Recommended Autac products: SJOW and SOW UL types provide oil and water resistance for wash-down environments. Our Auta-Prene thermoset rubber jacket compound handles the cleaning chemistries and food-acid exposures common in dairy, juice, and beverage operations. For dust-heavy nut and grain processing, shielded retractile configurations protect signal integrity on inspection and quality-monitoring equipment. Browse Coiled Cords for wash-down rated and shielded multi-conductor variants, and check the conductor color charts for plant-standard color coding.
