## Content Plan — Blog Posts (Priority Order)

### Phase 1: Quick Wins (Month 1–2)
Target keywords where Autac is positions 2–10 and a single well-optimized post can push to page 1.

#### Post 1: "Coiled Extension Cords: The Complete Guide to Retractile Power Cords"
- **Target keywords:** coiled extension cord (670/mo), coiled power cord (240/mo), coiled power cords, coiled ac power cord (40/mo)
- **Why:** Already position 3 for the highest-volume relevant keyword. One strong post could claim #1.
- **Content:** What they are, how they differ from standard extension cords, gauge/length selection guide, UL listing importance, applications (workshop, medical, industrial). Include a comparison table.
- **Internal links:** Link to products/coiled-cords.html, quote.html
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

#### Post 2: "Retractable Coil Cords: Buyer's Guide for OEMs & Purchasing Managers"
- **Target keywords:** retractable coil cord (90/mo), retractable coil cable (30/mo), retractable coil wire (20/mo)
- **Why:** Position 4–9 on several variants. Consolidate with one definitive post.
- **Content:** Specifications breakdown (AWG, conductors, voltage, UL types), how to choose the right cord, custom vs. stock, lead times, MOQs. Speak to the B2B buyer.
- **Internal links:** Link to products/retractile-cords.html, build-your-cord.html
- **Word count:** 1,200–1,500

#### Post 3: "What Is a Recoil Extension Cord? Everything You Need to Know"
- **Target keywords:** recoil extension cord (40/mo), extension cord recoil (10/mo)
- **Why:** Position 5, trending up. Easy win with targeted content.
- **Content:** Definition, how recoil mechanism works, use cases, safety considerations, Autac's product line. Answer "how to repair extension cord recoil" as a FAQ section.
- **Word count:** 1,000–1,200

#### Post 4: "Curly Phone Cords: Why They're Still Essential in Business & Healthcare"
- **Target keywords:** curly phone cord (120/mo), curly cords for phones (10/mo), spiral phone cord, no tangle desk phone cord (1,900/mo high competition)
- **Why:** Position 6 on curly phone cord with upward trend. Niche but relevant.
- **Content:** Why landline handset cords still matter (hospitals, call centers, hotels), cord length options, tangle-free solutions, replacement guide.
- **Internal links:** Link to products/curly-cords.html
- **Word count:** 1,000–1,200

### Phase 2: Authority Building (Month 2–4)
Longer-form content targeting higher-volume competitive keywords.

#### Post 5: "Retractable vs. Retractile Extension Cords: What's the Difference?"
- **Target keywords:** retractable extension cord (5,800/mo), retractable electrical cord (210/mo), retractable power cord
- **Why:** Massive search volume. Autac can own the educational angle — most searchers are looking for cord reels, but a well-crafted comparison post explaining the difference can capture informational traffic and redirect to Autac's actual products.
- **Content:** Clear distinction between retractable (reel-based, consumer) and retractile (coiled, industrial/commercial). Comparison table. "Which one do you actually need?" decision guide. This positions Autac as the authority.
- **Word count:** 1,800–2,200
- **Note:** This is the highest-volume play but most of the intent is consumer/cord-reel. Frame it as educational, not salesy.

#### Post 6: "Custom Coiled Cables: How to Spec and Order Your Perfect Cord"
- **Target keywords:** custom coiled cables (480/mo), custom electrical cables (30/mo), custom power cords (80/mo), custom made power cords (10/mo), custom length power cords (20/mo)
- **Why:** High combined volume across "custom" keywords. Build Your Cord page is already live — this blog post drives traffic to it.
- **Content:** Step-by-step guide to specifying a custom cord (material, gauge, conductors, length, terminations, shielding, UL requirements). Real examples of custom orders Autac has fulfilled. Include photos of the manufacturing process.
- **Internal links:** Heavy linking to build-your-cord.html
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

#### Post 7: "Spiral Cables: Types, Specifications & Industrial Applications"
- **Target keywords:** spiral cable (220/mo), spiral cord, spiral power cord (10/mo), spiral power cable (10/mo)
- **Why:** Not currently ranking. Medium volume with low KD (13). Easy to capture.
- **Content:** Overview of spiral cable types (power, data, USB, keyboard), material options, temperature ratings, industry applications (automotive, medical, telecom).
- **Word count:** 1,200–1,500

#### Post 8: "How Autac Manufactures Coil Cords: Inside a 77-Year-Old Connecticut Factory"
- **Target keywords:** coil cord manufacturer, coiled cord manufacturer, retractile cord manufacturer, woman owned manufacturer
- **Why:** Builds brand authority and E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust). Supports all product page rankings. Great for backlink acquisition.
- **Content:** Company history, manufacturing process (with photos/video), quality control, UL certification process, woman-owned story, made-in-USA differentiator.
- **Internal links:** Link to about.html, products.html
- **Word count:** 1,500–2,000

## Internal Linking Strategy

Every blog post should:
1. Link to **1–2 product pages** (the primary commercial page for that keyword cluster)
2. Link to **quote.html** or **build-your-cord.html** as CTA
3. Link to **1–2 other blog posts** (create a content web, not isolated posts)

Every product page should:
1. Link to **2–3 supporting blog posts** in a "Learn More" or "Related Articles" section
2. Cross-link to **other product category pages**

## Backlink Strategy (Brief)

The existing backlink profile is almost entirely low-quality spam links (blog comments, random social profiles). These need to be supplemented with legitimate links:

1. **Industry directories:** Thomas.net, GlobalSpec, IEC, NAED member directory
2. **Woman-owned business directories:** WBENC, SBA WOSB, WEConnect International
3. **Local/regional:** CT Manufacturing directory, CBIA, Branford Chamber of Commerce
4. **Manufacturing associations:** NAM, NEMA, UL listed manufacturer directories
5. **PR/Content:** Pitch the "77-year-old woman-owned manufacturer" story to trade publications (Wire Journal International, EC&M Magazine, Assembly Magazine)
