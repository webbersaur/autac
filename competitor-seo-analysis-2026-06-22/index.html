2. Keyword & content gaps

Themes competitors cover/rank for that Autac is thin or missing on.

Biggest gap

A. Dedicated per-industry / per-application pages

Competitors run individual landing pages per vertical; Autac has one /solutions/ hub listing 8 sectors — and even there Defense & Aerospace lacks detailed application examples. Build deep pages targeting "[application] coil cord" / "[industry] retractile cable":

  • Medical/dental, military & defense, aerospace/avionics, first responders (police/fire)
  • Airport ground support equipment (GSE), light tower cords, heavy/off-road equipment
  • Telecom, industrial automation/robotics
Highest ROI

B. Downloadable technical assets (you have none)

Your retractile page has no datasheets, PDF spec sheets, or design guides. Northwire offers a data sheet + 10-min video; Philatron and Specialty Wire publish catalog PDFs; Meridian leans on spec depth. These rank for "coil cord spec sheet / datasheet / catalog" and earn backlinks.

C. "How to design / choose / spec a coil cord" guides

Northwire's "4 Factors When Designing a Retractile Coil Cord" and Meridian's "How to Choose the Right Custom Coil Cord" own informational intent you could outrank. Target coil cord extension ratio, tangent direction, how to specify a retractile cord. You already explain the 1:5 ratio and manufacturing — repackage as standalone guides + a simple length/ratio calculator (linkable asset).

D. Materials & spec-code glossary depth

Competitors list cable codes (SOOW, SJOOW, SJT, SVT), strength members (Kevlar®, Spectra®, Teflon®, steel), and harder cycle ratings (100k–500k cycles, 100k 180° bend cycles). You cover TPE/TPR/PVC/Auta-Prene + 100k cycles — solid, but a materials/spec-code reference captures long-tail and supports E-E-A-T.

E. Synonyms & F. cable-assembly cluster

Northwire explicitly targets "slinkies" / "curly cords" — ensure informal synonyms (slinky cord, spiral cord, spring cord) appear naturally. Meridian & Cable Science rank for coiled cable assembly / overmolded coil cord; extend your /cord-sets/ page toward those terms.
