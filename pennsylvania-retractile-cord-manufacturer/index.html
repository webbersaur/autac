Industries We Serve Across Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania anchors one of the largest and most diversified manufacturing economies in the United States, with deep clusters that all generate steady demand for industrial retractile, curly, and coiled cordage. Rail and locomotive production centers on Erie. Steel, metals, and industrial machinery concentrate in the Pittsburgh region. Heavy-truck and process-equipment manufacturing fills the Lehigh Valley around Allentown. Pharmaceutical and life-sciences production clusters in Greater Philadelphia and West Point. And food and beverage processing — confectionery, snacks, and baked goods — spans Hershey, Hanover, and the south-central counties. Each cluster carries its own engineering requirements — current rating, jacket compound, flex life, shielding, environmental exposure — and the Autac product line maps directly to all five.

Rail, Locomotive & Transit Equipment

Pennsylvania is the heart of American rail manufacturing. Wabtec builds Evolution Series locomotives at its century-old Lawrence Park plant in Erie, one of the largest locomotive facilities in North America. Wabtec also operates rail-systems, braking, and transit-electronics operations from its Pittsburgh headquarters. The region's rail suppliers and rebuild shops support freight, transit, and passenger programs that demand durable, traceable power and signal cordage for assembly bays, test stands, and field-service rebuild kits — cordage that must survive heavy industrial environments and satisfy domestic-content and Buy American supplier requirements.

Typical Pennsylvania applications in this segment:

  • Locomotive final-assembly bay tool drops and power feeds
  • Traction-motor and braking-system test-stand cabling
  • Transit-car and rail-electronics assembly-line drops
  • Field-service and rebuild-shop retractile power cords
  • Domestic-content compliant cordage with a US-only supply chain

Recommended Autac products: TPR-jacketed retractile cords in 14 AWG and 12 AWG, 3-conductor / 15A configurations cover most assembly-line drops; custom retracted lengths from 18" to 36" are standard. Step up to 10 AWG / 20A for higher-current test-stand and rebuild applications. Browse the Retractile Cords hub for stock part numbers, or use the Build Your Cord tool to specify a custom configuration for your program. Autac is a US-only manufacturer with a US supply chain, which simplifies Buy American supplier qualification.

Steel, Metals & Industrial Machinery

The Pittsburgh region remains a national center for steel, metals, and heavy industrial equipment. U.S. Steel is headquartered in Pittsburgh and runs flat-rolled and tubular steel operations across the Mon Valley. PPG Industries manufactures coatings, specialty materials, and surface solutions from its Pittsburgh base. Kennametal produces cutting tools and tooling systems in Latrobe, and a deep ecosystem of metal-fabrication, machine-building, and additive-manufacturing shops fills the region. Metals and machinery production carries demanding cordage requirements: high-current power for mill and shop equipment, abrasion- and heat-resistant jackets for hot and dirty environments, and rugged retractile drops that pull cleanly out of moving overhead cranes and work cells.

Typical Pennsylvania applications in this segment:

  • Mill-floor and fabrication-shop high-current power drops
  • Overhead-crane and hoist pendant control cords
  • CNC, machine-tool, and welding-cell power and signal cords
  • Coating-line and finishing-station tool power
  • Heat- and abrasion-resistant retractile cords for hot work areas

Recommended Autac products: SO and SOW UL types with 12 AWG and 10 AWG conductors handle the high-current loads typical of mill and machine-shop equipment. Our Auta-Prene thermoset rubber jacket resists abrasion, oil, and heat in hot and dirty environments. Browse the Retractile Cords hub for rugged high-current configurations and Coiled Cords for shielded multi-conductor pendant and control assemblies. Check the conductor color charts for plant-standard wiring schemes.

Heavy Trucks, Engines & Process Equipment

Mack Trucks assembles all of its North American Class 8 trucks — including the LR Electric — at its 1.7-million-square-foot Lehigh Valley Operations plant in Macungie. Air Products, the global industrial-gas and process-equipment company, is headquartered in Allentown and engineers cryogenic and gas-processing systems there. B. Braun Medical operates large device and infusion-systems manufacturing in Allentown and Bethlehem. These operations run long moving assembly lines and test cells that need retractile drops to keep power and air tools off the floor and out of the work path, with jackets that resist cutting fluids, coolants, and shop chemistries.

Typical Pennsylvania applications in this segment:

  • Truck cab-and-chassis assembly-line tool drops on moving lines
  • Engine and powertrain test-cell power and instrumentation cabling
  • Process-skid and cryogenic-equipment build-station power
  • End-of-line inspection and torque-tool retractile cords
  • Device and infusion-systems assembly and packaging line power

Recommended Autac products: 14 AWG and 12 AWG retractile configurations cover most assembly-line drops, with custom retracted lengths and 1:3 to 1:5 extension ratios sized to each work cell. TPR and Auta-Prene jackets resist the cutting fluids and coolants common in heavy-equipment production. Browse the Retractile Cords hub for stock configurations, Cord Sets for custom-terminated assemblies, and use Build Your Cord to specify length, ratio, and termination for your line.

Pharmaceutical, Medical Device & Life Sciences

Greater Philadelphia is one of the country's foremost pharmaceutical corridors. Merck operates its largest manufacturing site in West Point, where roughly 9,500 employees make and test pharmaceuticals, vaccines, and biological products. GSK, Johnson & Johnson, and Teva Pharmaceuticals all maintain operations across the Philadelphia region, and B. Braun Medical manufactures infusion systems and devices in the Lehigh Valley. Pharmaceutical and device manufacturing carries strict constraints: ISO-classified cleanroom controls, sterilization-compatible jacket compounds, FDA documentation and traceability, and zero tolerance for particulate contamination on production lines.

Typical Pennsylvania applications in this segment:

  • Cleanroom and aseptic-fill line assembly tool drops
  • Vaccine and biologics process-suite power and control cords
  • Sterilization-compatible cord assemblies for reusable equipment
  • Packaging, labeling, and serialization-line tool power
  • Diagnostic and device assembly-station signal and power cables

Recommended Autac products: Our Auta-Prene thermoset rubber and TPR jacket compounds resist the cleaning and sterilization chemistries common in pharmaceutical manufacturing. For signal applications, 18 AWG and 16 AWG shielded retractile and curly configurations support millions of flex cycles on automated assembly and inspection equipment. Browse Curly Cords for high-flex signal builds and Coiled Cords for shielded multi-conductor configurations. Lot traceability documentation is available for FDA submissions.

Food, Beverage & Confectionery Processing

South-central Pennsylvania is one of the nation's densest food-processing regions. The Hershey Company runs chocolate processing and confectionery production in Hershey, including a new fully integrated digital chocolate plant. Utz Brands and Snyder's of Hanover anchor the York County "Snack Food Capital," producing chips, pretzels, and salty snacks at scale. Bimbo Bakeries USA, headquartered in Horsham, operates high-volume bakery lines across the state. These operations share a common environment: wash-down sanitation cycles, sugar and food-acid exposure, dust and particulate from grain and ingredient handling, and zero tolerance for line downtime during peak production runs.

Typical Pennsylvania applications in this segment:

  • Wash-down rated retractile drops over packaging and filling lines
  • Palletizer, case-packer, and depalletizer tool power
  • Conveyor-line inspection station and torque-tool power
  • Confectionery and bakery oven-line and enrobing-station cordage
  • Snack-line dust-environment shielded inspection equipment cords

Recommended Autac products: SJOW and SOW UL types provide oil and water resistance for wash-down environments. Our Auta-Prene thermoset rubber jacket compound handles the cleaning chemistries and food-acid exposures common in confectionery, snack, and bakery operations. For dust-heavy snack and grain processing, shielded retractile configurations protect signal integrity on inspection and quality-monitoring equipment. Browse Coiled Cords for wash-down rated and shielded multi-conductor variants, and check the conductor color charts for plant-standard color coding.
