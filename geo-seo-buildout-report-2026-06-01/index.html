Why this build-out. Following the recent www / non-www migration issue that affected the site’s search visibility, this work was completed to rebuild the lost ground — a substantial content and technical expansion provided at no additional cost. Everything below is net-new: pages and posts the site did not have before, built to recover and grow organic reach.

1. What was built

Per-state geo-SEO landing pages plus two supporting blog posts each, all cloned from the existing California template (byte-identical shell, researched per-state content), framed as “manufactured in North Branford, CT — shipped to {state}.”

  • 15 state landing pages at /{state}-retractile-cord-manufacturer/ with per-state industries, named OEMs, cities, freight times, and Breadcrumb / Service / FAQPage JSON-LD.
  • New /service-areas/ hub linking all 17 state pages (incl. CA + WI), grouped by region.
  • 30 blog posts (one industry deep-dive + one sourcing guide per state), each linking back to its state page and the product hubs.
  • All wired into sitemap.xml, blog/index.html, and the GSC indexing worklist.

4. Automation

Local launchd job com.autac.publish-blog-batch — runs Mondays 9am local, releases the next 3 posts (restamps dates, wires them in), commits, and pushes (Vercel auto-deploys). Driver: seo-reports/publish_blog_batch.sh + publish_blog_batch.py; queue: seo-reports/blog-publish-queue.txt. Logs to ~/Library/Logs/autac-publish-blog.log.
Cloud routine disabled. A cloud /schedule routine was created and tested but its checkout is read-only for this repo — it cannot git push. It was disabled in favor of the local job (which uses working osxkeychain credentials).
